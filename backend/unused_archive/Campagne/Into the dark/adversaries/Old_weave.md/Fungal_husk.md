## FUNGAL HUSK

Tier 2 Bruiser

At first glance, a dead sellsword sprawled in the webbing. Closer inspection reveals pale fungal filaments replacing their tendons, a bulbous orange growth where their heart should be, and—too late—eyes snapping open.

Motives & Tactics: Ambush the curious, spread spores, drag prey into darkness, protect the mycelium network

Difficulty: 15 | Thresholds: 14/27 | HP: 8 | Stress: 5
ATK: +1 | Fungal Claw: Melee | 2d8+4 phy

Experience: Corpse Mimicry +3, Unflinching +2

# FEATURES

Playing Dead – Passive: The Fungal Husk appears to be a normal corpse. It does not register as hostile or animate. The first PC who moves into Melee range to inspect or loot the body triggers the Husk's Spore Burst reaction automatically, and the Husk gains a surprise round (spotlight immediately).

Spore Burst – Reaction: When a PC moves into Melee range of the Husk for the first time (or when the Husk is first damaged), the Husk releases a cloud of necrotic spores. All creatures within Very Close range must make a Might Reaction Roll (15) . On a failure, they take 1d10 magic damage and become Poisoned (disadvantage on attack rolls until the end of the scene or until they spend an action to purge the spores). On a success, they take half damage and mark 1 Stress.

Fungal Frenzy – Action: Mark 1 Stress to make a standard attack with advantage. On a success, deal an additional 1d8 direct physical damage as the fungus surges, pushing the corpse beyond natural limits.

Mycelial Anchor – Passive: The Husk cannot be knocked Prone or moved against its will by physical force. The fungal threads anchoring it to the ground are stronger than steel wire.

Death Rattle – Reaction: When the Husk is reduced to 0 HP, it does not immediately die. Instead, it takes one final action (any action from its stat block) before collapsing. After that action, the orange heart-growth bursts, releasing a final Spore Burst (as above) centered on the Husk.
