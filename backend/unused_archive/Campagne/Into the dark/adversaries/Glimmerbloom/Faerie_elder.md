## FAERIE ELDER

Tier 2 Social

An ancient fey wrapped in silver-blue robes that shift like moonlight on water, their skin faintly luminous, their hair threaded with living moss. Their shadow has eight points, though no source of light explains why. Their voice is a half-remembered lullaby, and their eyes hold the weight of centuries—kind, but measuring.

Motives & Tactics: Protect the village above all, test strangers before trusting, share knowledge for a price, preserve fading traditions, dream the roots into wakefulness

Difficulty: 16 | Thresholds: 12/20 | HP: 8 | Stress: 6
ATK: +0 | Root-Touched Staff: Melee | 1d8+2 mag (non-lethal unless provoked)

Experience: Local Lore +4, Dreamweaving +3, Fey Etiquette +3

# FEATURES

Dreamer of Glimmerbloom – Passive
The Faerie Elder dreamed Glimmerbloom into existence a century ago. While they sleep beneath the central root-chamber, the village persists. If they are killed or driven mad, the village begins to unravel—roots wither, lights fade, and the protective wards fail within 1d4 days. The villagers know this. They would die for the Elder.

What happens to a dreamer when the dream ends? Does the Elder dream because they are trapped, or are they trapped because they dream?

Voice of the Roots – Passive
The Elder can feel anything that touches Glimmerbloom's root network—trespassers, injured animals, even spilled blood. While within the village, they are aware of any significant disturbance (combat, theft, magical surge) within moments. They cannot be surprised in their own home.

How many intruders have the roots claimed before the Elder even rose from their chair?

The Elder's Test – Action
Spend a Fear to have the Elder pose a moral or philosophical question to a specific PC, drawn from their century of watching strangers come and go. Examples:

"You found a wounded wolf and a lost lamb. Which do you save?"

"A promise made to the wicked—must it be kept?"

"What would you sacrifice to save a stranger?"

Wrath of the Dreamer – Reaction (Fear Feature)
If the Elder is attacked or witnesses a villager being harmed, they can spend 2 Fear to activate this reaction. The roots of Glimmerbloom surge to life. All creatures the Elder chooses within Close range must make a Might Reaction Roll (19) . On a failure, they are Restrained (escape DC 15, action to attempt) and take 1d10 magic damage as roots tighten around them. On a success, they break free but mark 1 Stress. The Elder does not attack again—they simply say: "Leave. Now. While I still remember mercy."

When was the last time the Elder showed this mercy? What happened to those who did not receive it?
