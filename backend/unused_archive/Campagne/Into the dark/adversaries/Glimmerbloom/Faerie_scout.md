## FAERIE SCOUT

Tier 2 Ranged

A lean, sharp-eyed faerie clad in woven reeds and fallen leaves, moving through the upper branches without a sound. They watch the forest approaches and return to the village with warnings—or arrows.

Motives & Tactics: Observe from distance, warn the village, harry pursuers, never fight fair

Difficulty: 14 | Thresholds: 8/16 | HP: 5 | Stress: 4
ATK: +3 | Thornbow: Far | 2d8+4 phy

Experience: Forest Navigation +3, Silent Movement +3

# FEATURES

Canopy Perch – Passive: The Scout begins combat at Far range and at least Close range vertically (in branches, on a root-bridge, etc.). Melee attacks against them have disadvantage unless the attacker can reach their elevation.

Warning Shot – Action: Make an attack against a target within Far range. On a success, deal 2d6 physical damage and the target must succeed on a Presence Reaction Roll (14) or become Frightened of the Scout until the end of their next turn.

Vanish – Action: Mark 1 Stress to become Hidden until they attack or a PC succeeds on an Instinct roll (14) to find them. While Hidden, their next attack has advantage.

Forest Warning – Reaction: When the Scout first detects an intruder (or when combat begins), you can mark 1 Stress to alert up to 1d4 other Faerie allies within Far range. Those allies are not surprised and may act normally on the first GM turn.
