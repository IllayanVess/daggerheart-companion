## HISTORY KEEPER

Tier 2 Support

An ancient, stooped faerie with blind white eyes and a voice like dry leaves, their fingers tracing carved root-tablets that hold the village's memories. They remember what others have forgotten—and what others wish to forget.

Motives & Tactics: Preserve truth, guide the worthy, never lie, trade memory for memory

Difficulty: 15 | Thresholds: 7/14 | HP: 5 | Stress: 6
ATK: +0 | Memory Sting: Close | 1d8 mag (non-lethal)

Experience: Ancient Lore +4, Memory Weaver +3, Truth Seeker +3

# FEATURES

Blind Seer – Passive: The History Keeper cannot see, but they perceive through the roots. They are aware of any creature within Close range regardless of invisibility, hiding, or darkness. They cannot be surprised.

Share Memory – Action: The History Keeper touches a willing (or restrained) creature and shares a specific memory. The target must make a Spirit Reaction Roll (15). On a failure, they are Stunned (lose next action) as the memory overwhelms them. On a success, they gain advantage on their next Lore or Instinct roll related to that memory's subject. The History Keeper may use this to reveal clues, teach skills, or interrogate prisoners.

Borrowed Time – Action: Mark 1 Stress to grant one ally within Close range a flash of prophetic insight. That ally gains advantage on their next action roll. However, after that roll, the ally marks 1 Stress as the vision fades.

Root-Tablet Ward – Reaction: When an ally within Very Close range would take damage, the History Keeper can mark 1 Stress to raise a carved root-tablet as a shield. Reduce that damage by 1d8. Describe how the tablet cracks but protects.

The Unwritten Truth – Passive (Fear Feature): Once per scene, when a PC asks the History Keeper a direct question about the past, you may spend 1 Fear to have them answer with complete honesty—but the answer reveals something the PC did not want to hear. The PC who asked the question must mark 1 Stress as they process an uncomfortable truth.
